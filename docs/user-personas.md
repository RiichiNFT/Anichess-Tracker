# Anichess Tracker — User Personas

---

## Persona 1: The Web2 Competitive Chess Player

### "The Lichess Regular"

**Name:** Marcus, 26  
**Location:** Europe (active in online chess communities)  
**Background:** Has been playing chess online for 5+ years. Maintains a 1600–1900 Lichess rapid rating. Competes in Lichess arena tournaments weekly. Follows top streamers and has a strong grasp of openings, tactics, and rating systems.

### Goals
- Evaluate whether Anichess is worth investing time in — does skill transfer?
- Understand the ELO system and how their rating compares to other players
- Track their own ranking progress as they climb from newcomer to competitive status
- Qualify for the Rising Stars finals and win prize money
- Understand the tournament bracket and who they'd face

### Behaviours on This Site
- Checks the leaderboard daily to monitor their rank vs. known rivals
- Looks at the **Top 8 cutoff line** to gauge how close they are to qualifying
- Reads the **tournament details** carefully — rule specifics like spell uniqueness rules, BO3 format, and tiebreak procedures matter to them
- Monitors the **wild card (Top 2 matches)** section when they're grinding games to qualify
- Checks the bracket to understand seedings and potential matchups
- May not fully understand wallet addresses — prefers to see usernames

### Pain Points
- Unfamiliar with web3 concepts (wallets, CHECK tokens, signing)
- Confused by wallet addresses instead of usernames in the leaderboard
- Wants to know: "Is my Anichess rating calculated the same way as FIDE/Lichess ELO?"
- May be unsure how the spell system interacts with chess strategy

### What Would Win Them Over
- Clear tier labels (Pawn, Knight, ... Legend) that map to skill, similar to Lichess titles
- Easy-to-read countdown to know exactly when the qualifying window closes
- Transparent qualification criteria so they know exactly what to aim for
- A visible link to their Anichess profile from the leaderboard

## Persona 2: The Active Anichess Player

### "The Early Adopter"

**Name:** Priya, 22  
**Location:** Southeast Asia (HKT timezone)  
**Background:** Discovered Anichess through the Anichess Discord or a crypto/gaming community. Has played 100–300 ranked games. Holds a web3 wallet and has connected it to the platform. Understands spell mechanics and has developed preferred spell loadouts. May have moderate traditional chess skills but excels at the spell-game layer.

### Goals
- Win CHECK tokens — both through tournament prizes and rating milestone rewards
- Stay informed about where they rank in the newcomer pool
- Track how many games they've played since the wild card period opened (May 11)
- Confirm their spot in the Rising Stars Finals before the cutoff
- Keep an eye on which other players are rising quickly

### Behaviours on This Site
- Visits the site multiple times a day during active grinding periods
- Immediately checks the **Top 2 · Most Matches** wild card section to see if they're in the top 2
- Watches the **CUT OFF IN** countdown closely — plans gaming sessions around it
- Cross-references their Anichess wallet address against the leaderboard
- Checks the **absent** status tags to see if rivals will forfeit their spot
- Looks at the bracket to understand tournament format and prize allocation

### Pain Points
- Rating can feel unpredictable — wants to understand why it went up or down
- Concerned about substitution rules: "What if a top-8 player doesn't confirm?"
- Worried about timezone confusion (events are in UTC, they're in HKT)
- May have registered but isn't sure if their wallet is being tracked

### What Would Win Them Over
- Real-time data that matches what they see on Anichess.com
- Visible wallet address → username mapping so they can quickly find themselves
- Clear communication of the absence/substitution system
- The "CLIMB FOR REWARDS" tag linking to rating milestone rewards — directly relevant to their earning goal

## Shared Traits Across Both Personas

| Trait | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Competitive mindset** | Both track standings obsessively during active qualifying periods |
| **Mobile users** | Likely checking on phones between gaming sessions |
| **Community-driven** | Found the tracker through Discord or community links, not organic search |
| **Time-sensitive** | Both personas are highly responsive to countdowns and deadlines |
| **Trust through transparency** | They trust the site more when data sources, cutoff rules, and criteria are clearly explained |

---

## Key Design Implications

- **Leaderboard must resolve wallet → username** — raw addresses are a barrier for both personas
- **Countdown visibility is critical** — the "CUT OFF IN" timer is the most action-driving element on the page
- **Mobile-first matters** — both personas check standings on their phones
- **Qualification criteria must be self-explanatory** — users should not need to visit Discord to understand how to qualify
- **Prize structure should be prominently visible** — it's the primary motivation for active grinding
